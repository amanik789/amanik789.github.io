AMAN KARKI

I am a highly motivated structural engineer currently pursuing a Master of Science in Structural Engineering at Oklahoma State University, with an expected graduation in May 2026. I have a strong foundation in structural performance assessment and the design of earthquake resistant buildings, with a hands on experience on softwares such as ETABS, SAP2000, IDEA Statica, Autocad and so on. As a graduate research assistant, I have worked on developing numerical models that are capable of predicting extreme limit states of structures and quantification of associated uncertainties in predicting seismic collapse capacities. I am a highly motivated engineer who works effectively both independently and in collaborative environments, with a strong desire to continuously learn, take on challenging problems, and contribute to meaningful engineering projects.

Besides design, research and teaching, I like practicing martial arts, weight training, long-distance running, hiking, travelling, listening to informative podcasts (such as Peter Attia Drive, Huberman Lab, etc.) and reading books (such as Man's Search for Meaning by Victor Frankl, Beyond Good and Evil by Friedrich Nietzsche, etc.)
